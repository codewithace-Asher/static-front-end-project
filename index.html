Price Action


Price action is a technique used in technical analysis that examines the movement of security prices over time, rather than relying on indicators or other forms of analysis. It looks at the price changes on charts, and uses patterns and trends to make predictions about future movements.Price action traders believe that the study of past price movements can help predict future price movements.


They look for patterns, trends, and other market characteristics that can provide clues about the direction of the market.The basic idea behind price action is that market participants, such as traders and investors, leave their footprints on charts in the form of price movements.

Market Structure


Market structure on a chart is like detective work, where traders and investors examine the footprints of market participants in the form of price movements, trends, patterns and volume to uncover clues about the underlying sentiment and direction of the market, but as in every detective story, you should always keep in mind that all clues must be analyzed and cross-referenced with other data to truly understand the bigger picture.


It is important to note that market structure analysis is not a perfect or infallible method and can be affected by unforeseen circumstances. Therefore, it's a good idea to combine it with other methods of analysis and not to rely on it solely.


Fibonacci


Fibonacci retracements use key levels determined by the Fibonacci sequence to identify potential levels of support and resistance on a stock chart and often used in conjunction with other indicators to provide a comprehensive view of the market.


Fibonacci retracements are like a treasure map for traders, they use the ancient mathematical sequence to uncover hidden levels of support and resistance on a stock chart, acting as potential pivots points on the journey to uncovering profits, but remember to use it along with other tools and analysis, to make sure you are not following a false map.
